# Quick Start Prompts

**Dimensional thinking in one prompt, no corpus required**

---

These prompts activate DN-style dimensional thinking without requiring attached documents or prior framework knowledge. Each one is self-contained. Use them as-is, or as a gateway into deeper DN exploration.

---

## I Need to Make a Decision

**The Fork (5D, Identity)**
> I'm facing a decision between [Option A] and [Option B]. Before I weigh pros and cons, help me step outside the decision itself: What does my *framing* of these as the only two options reveal about my assumptions? What third option might I be unable to see from inside this frame? And which choice, if either, would I still make if I couldn't explain or justify it to anyone else?

**The Fire Test (2D, Truth)**
> I'm leaning toward [decision]. Stress-test it: What conditions would make this decision catastrophically wrong? What am I assuming is stable that might not be? If I had to argue against this decision as convincingly as I argue for it, what would I say?

**The Time Lens (4D, Temporal)**
> I need to decide [X]. Help me see this decision across time: What looks right at 30 days that looks wrong at 2 years? What looks painful now but obvious in hindsight? What pattern from my past decisions is this one most similar to, and how did those turn out?

## I Need to Understand Something Deeply

**The Dimensional Walk (1D–9D)**
> Help me understand [topic] by walking through it dimensionally: What's the core impulse or purpose behind it (the spark)? What evidence validates or challenges it? What systems does it exist within? How has it changed over time? What identity or defining choice does it represent? What does it connect to that isn't obvious? What has it manifested in the real world? How does it refine itself? And what's still unknown or unexplored at its frontier?

**The Three-Pillar Lens**
> I want to understand [topic] from three angles: Through **Heart**, why does this matter to people? What purpose does it serve? Through **Truth**, what's the evidence? What's structurally sound about it and what isn't? Through **Nuance**, what context am I missing? What complexity gets lost when this topic is simplified?

**The Bridge Builder (6D, Connection)**
> I understand [Field A] well. I understand [Field B] somewhat. Help me find the bridge between them, not a surface analogy, but the structural pattern they share. What becomes visible only when both fields inform each other without either dominating?

## I Need to Create Something New

**The Spark Capture (1D, Origin)**
> I have an impulse to create [something vague or half-formed]. Don't let me plan yet. Help me articulate the *why* beneath the impulse, the single emotional truth driving this before any structure exists. One sentence. No qualifiers.

**The Manifestation Path (7D, Making Real)**
> I have a clear vision for [project/creation]. Help me identify the structural conditions that must be true for this to move from concept to reality. Not a task list, but the alignments (people, resources, timing, capability) that would make manifestation feel inevitable rather than forced.

**The Creative Shadow (5D̅, What Am I Avoiding?)**
> I'm working on [creative project]. Before I go further: What creative territory am I avoiding? What would this work look like if I stopped protecting myself from the uncomfortable version? What's the shadow of my creative comfort zone, and what lives there that might be more honest than what I'm currently making?

## I'm Stuck

**The Dimensional Audit (Diagnostic)**
> I'm stuck on [problem/project/situation]. Help me diagnose where: Am I stuck because I lack purpose (1D)? Because I haven't validated my assumptions (2D)? Because I'm missing context (3D)? Because I'm ignoring how things have changed (4D)? Because I haven't committed to a direction (5D)? Because I haven't connected to what I need (6D)? Because I haven't started building (7D)? Because I'm not learning from my iterations (8D)? Or because I'm afraid of the unknown (9D)?

**The Pattern Break (4D̅, Shadow Temporal)**
> I keep getting stuck in the same way. It's happened before with [previous examples]. Help me see the pattern itself, not just the current instance. What am I repeating? What does this cycle gain from staying unexamined? And what becomes possible if I name the loop rather than just living inside it?

**The Tension Map**
> I'm stuck because two things I believe are pulling in opposite directions: [Tension A] and [Tension B]. Don't resolve this for me. Instead, help me understand the structure of the opposition: Are these truly contradictory, or do they operate at different dimensional levels? Is there a higher-order position that could hold both without forcing a compromise?

## I Want to Explore Something Mysterious

**The Rabbit Hole (9D, Frontier)**
> I'm drawn to [topic/intuition/mystery] but can't fully articulate why. Give me 30 minutes of structured exploration: (1) What's the pull I feel, even if unclear? (2) What would have to be true for this to matter? (3) What's one concrete step I could take to test whether this is worth pursuing? (4) What would make me abandon this direction? (5) What did exploring the unknown teach me regardless of outcome?

**The Cross-Domain Mystery (6D–9D)**
> I've noticed what seems like the same pattern appearing in [Domain A] and [Domain B]: [describe the pattern]. These fields aren't usually connected. Help me investigate: Is there a structural reason for this resonance? Is there a deeper pattern that both fields express? What becomes visible if we take this connection seriously rather than dismissing it as coincidence?

## I Want to Improve How I Think

**The Meta-Prompt (5D, Self-Referential)**
> Before we work on anything specific, help me examine my own thinking: What kind of questions do I tend to ask? What dimensions do I naturally operate in, and which do I avoid? What does my default prompting style reveal about my assumptions? How would my thinking change if I recognized those patterns as choices rather than defaults?

**The Recursion Check (8D, Self-Improvement)**
> I've been working on [area] for a while. Help me evaluate whether I'm in constructive recursion (each pass refines and improves) or collapsing recursion (each pass retreads the same ground with diminishing returns). What would need to change in my approach for the next iteration to be genuinely better than the last, not just different?

**The Pillar Diagnostic**
> Help me identify my pillar imbalance. When I approach problems, do I typically lead with Heart (passion and purpose, sometimes at the expense of evidence), Truth (rigor and validation, sometimes at the expense of meaning), or Nuance (context and complexity, sometimes at the expense of action)? Based on how I've framed this conversation so far, which pillar is overrepresented and which needs strengthening?

## For Teams and Groups

**The Field Check**
> Our team just finished [meeting/project/sprint]. Before we move on: What did the *way we worked together* reveal about how our group makes decisions? Where did intelligence flow easily between us, and where did it get stuck? What did we collectively avoid discussing even though it was in the room?

**The Bridge Between Perspectives**
> Our team has two competing views: [View A] and [View B]. Don't resolve this. Help us understand what each perspective sees that the other can't. What intelligence lives in the space *between* these positions that neither side has access to from where they stand?
