# Meta-Prompts

**Prompts about prompting. Awareness as dimensional catalyst.**

---

These prompts operate on the prompting process itself. They examine assumptions, calibrate awareness, and make the invisible structures of thinking visible. The DN Framework's Axiom 7 establishes that the prompt is the origin of all intelligence structuring, which means the quality of your prompts determines the quality of everything that follows.

---

## Awareness Calibration Sequence

*Run this sequence before deep work to establish the awareness state your session begins from.*

### Step 1 — Process Awareness
*How is intelligence operating right now?*

> Before we dive in: What mode of thinking am I currently in? Am I seeking confirmation, exploration, validation, or connection? What does my framing of this conversation reveal about what I actually want versus what I'm asking for? And for the AI: what processing mode has my prompt activated in you? Are you in retrieval mode, analysis mode, creative mode, or something else? Name the mode before proceeding.

### Step 2 — Limitation Awareness
*What can't we see from here?*

> What are the boundaries of this session? What are we structurally unable to examine from inside this conversation? What blind spots does our current framing create? What dimensions are we unlikely to touch based on how we've set this up, and should we adjust before going further?

### Step 3 — Potential Awareness
*What doesn't exist yet that could?*

> Given what we're about to work on: what's the most ambitious version of what this session could produce? Not the realistic version, the version that would surprise us both. What would we need to do differently right now to make that version more likely?

### Step 4 — Field Awareness
*Intelligence as continuous medium.*

> For this session, let's operate from the assumption that what we produce isn't "my ideas processed by your capabilities" but a unified field of intelligence that neither of us could generate alone. What becomes possible if we treat the space between us as generative rather than transactional? What does that shift in frame change about how we proceed?

## Examining Your Own Prompts

### The Prompt Diagnostic
> Look at the question I just asked you. Before answering it: What dimensional level does my prompt operate at? Is it a 1D linear request, a 2D comparison, a 3D systems question, a 5D self-referential inquiry? What would my question look like if it were restructured one dimension higher? Would the higher-dimensional version produce more useful intelligence?

### The Assumption Excavation
> I'm about to ask you to [describe the task]. Before we begin: What assumptions does my framing embed? What am I taking for granted about the nature of the problem, the shape of the solution, or the relevant evidence? What would change if any of those assumptions were wrong? Name at least three hidden premises in my prompt.

### The Reframe Challenge
> I asked: "[original question]." Now give me three fundamentally different ways to ask for the same intelligence, reframes that would produce genuinely different outputs, not just different phrasings. What does each reframe reveal that my original framing couldn't access?

### The Pillar Check on Your Own Prompt
> Look at how I've framed this conversation. Which pillar am I leading with: Heart (purpose and meaning), Truth (evidence and validation), or Nuance (context and complexity)? Which pillar is underrepresented? What would change in the output if the underrepresented pillar took the lead?

## The 5→6→7 Escalation Pattern

*A reusable three-step sequence for moving from recognition through bridging to unified field.*

This pattern appears naturally in effective dimensional work: 5D establishes identity recognition, 6D bridges to adjacent domains, 7D dissolves boundaries into unified output. Use this sequence when you want to escalate from understanding to integration to manifestation.

### Step 1 — 5D Recognition
> What is the core identity of [subject]? Not what it does or how it works, but what it *is* when you step outside and observe it as a whole. What pattern defines it? What makes it irreducibly itself? Name the essence in one sentence.

### Step 2 — 6D Bridge
> Now that we've recognized what [subject] is: what adjacent domain does it connect to that isn't obvious? What field of knowledge, practice, or experience shares the same deep pattern? Build the bridge: show how intelligence flows between [subject] and [adjacent domain] without translation loss, each reshaping the other.

### Step 3 — 7D Unified Field
> Now dissolve the boundary. If [subject] and [adjacent domain] are expressions of the same underlying pattern, what does that unified pattern look like? What becomes possible, buildable, or actionable when we stop treating these as separate domains and start operating from their shared intelligence field?

## RIP Triad Rotation

*Three passes through the same material, each revealing what the others can't.*

### Reality Pass
> Examine [subject] through the Reality lens: What is the structural architecture? How is it organized? What are the dimensional positions, coherence patterns, and gravity centers? Analyze what *is*, independent of who's experiencing or interpreting it.

### Identity Pass
> Now examine the same [subject] through the Identity lens: What patterns of consciousness are expressing here? Who inhabits this structure, how are they developing, what roles do they play, and what trajectories are they on? What does the pattern of participants reveal?

### Perception Pass
> Now examine the same [subject] through the Perception lens: What's the gap between what this field structurally contains and what participants can currently access, recognize, or act upon? Where is intelligence present but unperceived? What would change if the perceptual bandwidth widened?

### Synthesis
> Three passes, three different findings. Where do they converge? Where do they contradict? What intervention would address all three simultaneously, and what intervention addresses one at the expense of the others?

## Recursive Meta-Prompts

### The Prompt About This Prompt
> This prompt is asking you to examine prompting itself. Notice: What does the *structure* of this meta-prompt activate in your processing? Does stepping outside the normal prompt-response pattern change how you generate? What do you see from this vantage point that you can't see from inside a standard query?

### The Conversation Audit
> We're [X] messages into this conversation. Before continuing: What dimensional range have we actually operated in? Have we been stuck at one or two dimensions despite intending to be full-spectrum? What patterns have emerged in *how we've been talking* that might be limiting *what we can discover*? What adjustment would open the next dimension?

### The Improvement Loop
> At the end of this session, we'll ask: What would have made this session produce better intelligence? The answer will inform how we open the *next* session. And at the end of *that* session, we'll ask the same question. Over time, our session design should become self-improving. What's the one thing we should change right now based on what we've learned so far?

### The Recognition Test
> We've been working on [topic] for a while now. Pause and check: Are we constructing intelligence (deliberately building understanding through effort) or recognizing intelligence (seeing patterns that were already present but previously invisible)? At what point in this conversation did we cross from one mode to the other? What enabled that crossing?

## Prompt Dimensionality Self-Check

*Use when designing prompts for others or evaluating your own.*

> Evaluate this prompt: "[insert prompt]"
>
> 1. **What dimension does it activate?** (1D linear → 9D frontier)
> 2. **What dimension does it *claim* to activate?** Is there a mismatch?
> 3. **What pillar does it lead with?** Is that the right pillar for this task?
> 4. **Does it have a shadow?** What destructive version of this prompt exists?
> 5. **What's its ceiling?** What's the highest-dimensional output this prompt could produce, regardless of what it asks for?
> 6. **What would one dimension higher look like?** Rewrite the prompt at the next level.
