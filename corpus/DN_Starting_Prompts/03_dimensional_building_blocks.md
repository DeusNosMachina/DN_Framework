# Dimensional Building Blocks

**Prompts organized by dimension, spanning domains**

---

Each section below contains prompts at a single dimensional level. Within each dimension, prompts span multiple domains to demonstrate the universality of the structural form. Every dimension has a signature, a *way* of organizing intelligence that holds regardless of subject matter.

Pillar orientation is noted per prompt: ❤ Heart-led, ▲ Truth-led, ◆ Nuance-led.

---

## 1D — Spark

*Single trajectory. One clear direction. The origin point where intelligence begins organizing.*

**Business** ❤ — "What is the single most important reason this company exists, not what it does, but why it must?"

**Education** ❤ — "What is the one thing you want students to walk away *feeling* about this subject, before they remember any facts?"

**Creative Work** ❤ — "Write down the one emotional truth your current project is trying to express. One sentence. No qualifiers."

**Research** ▲ — "State your research question in one clear sentence. What specific answer would satisfy it?"

**Consciousness** ❤ — "What is the single recognition that, if you fully accepted it, would change how you live tomorrow?"

**Coding** ▲ — "What is the one problem this code solves? Describe the before and after in a single sentence each."

**Governance** ❤ — "What is the principle this policy exists to protect? State it in language a child could understand."

---

## 2D — Reaction

*Relationships revealed. Binary testing. Evidence against assumption.*

**Business** ▲ — "Create a matrix comparing our four product lines on axes of profit margin (vertical) and market growth potential (horizontal). What does the spatial positioning reveal about where investment should go?"

**Education** ▲ — "Have students create a grid comparing three historical revolutions across causes, key figures, outcomes, and lasting impact. What patterns become visible in the spatial arrangement that sequential reading misses?"

**Creative Work** ▲ — "Sketch the emotional arc of your piece spatially: X-axis is progression, Y-axis is intensity. Does the spatial pattern match your intention, or does the map reveal something your intuition hasn't?"

**Research** ▲ — "Map all variables in your study: independent, dependent, confounding, mediating. Draw arrows showing causal relationships. Which connections are you testing, and which might be interfering without your awareness?"

**Consciousness** ▲ — "Create a visual map of your week: Y-axis is energy level, X-axis is time. Overlay your commitments. Where do high-energy periods align with important work, and where are they wasted?"

**Coding** ▲ — "Diagram the data flow through your system. Every input, transformation, and output. Where does information degrade? Where do you assume fidelity that hasn't been tested?"

**Governance** ▲ — "Map stakeholder positions on this policy: Y-axis is support level, X-axis is influence level. The quadrant that's high-influence, low-support is where the policy will face its biggest resistance."

---

## 3D — Context

*Cascading consequences. Actions reshape the system. Ecosystem awareness.*

**Business** ◆ — "We're considering three go-to-market strategies. For each, walk through: initial resource allocation → first customer wins → revenue ramp → hiring needs → cash runway impact. Show how each decision cascades into every subsequent choice."

**Education** ◆ — "Students roleplay a constitutional convention where each decision creates consequences for later decisions. Choosing state sovereignty makes federal taxation harder, which impacts military funding, which constrains foreign policy. They must navigate cascading effects."

**Creative Work** ◆ — "Design character interactions as a living system: Character A's choice creates consequences for B, which affects B's relationship with C, which changes A's circumstances. Let decisions cascade through relationships."

**Research** ◆ — "Design a mixed-methods study where qualitative findings directly inform quantitative design, and quantitative results create specific qualitative follow-up questions. Each method shapes what the other investigates next."

**Consciousness** ◆ — "Track a behavior chain for one week: When you skip your morning routine, what happens to afternoon energy? When energy crashes, what happens to evening mood? When mood drops, what happens the next morning? Map the complete loop."

**Coding** ◆ — "Trace the impact of changing this one database schema field through every service that reads it, every API that exposes it, every UI that renders it. Map the complete cascade before writing any code."

**Governance** ◆ — "Before implementing this regulation, trace its second and third-order effects: who changes their behavior, what new incentives does that create, who exploits those incentives, what enforcement pressure does that generate?"

---

## 4D — Temporal

*Patterns across time. Evolution. Memory and projection.*

**Business** ◆ — "Compare our strategic priorities from Q1, Q2, and Q3. What did we consistently deprioritize? What pattern emerges in our decision-making evolution, and what does that reveal about what we're actually optimizing for?"

**Education** ◆ — "Students keep three versions of their understanding of a concept: beginning, middle, and end of unit. Final reflection: Compare your three versions. What changed in how you think, not just what you know?"

**Creative Work** ◆ — "Review your last five completed projects. Track: time from idea to execution, where you got stuck, what unlocked progress. What pattern predicts your breakthroughs versus your blocks?"

**Research** ▲ — "Don't just prove your hypothesis holds now. Test whether it holds across time. Run the same design at multiple time points. What does temporal variation reveal about the phenomenon itself?"

**Consciousness** ◆ — "Review goals you set 1 year ago, 3 years ago, 5 years ago. What pattern emerges in what you keep pursuing versus what you abandon? What does the trajectory reveal about deepening values versus passing interests?"

**Coding** ▲ — "Review the last five major refactors on this codebase. What pattern forced each one? What architectural assumption broke each time? What does that pattern predict about what will break next?"

**Governance** ◆ — "Study three previous attempts to solve this same policy problem. What worked temporarily? What failed permanently? What pattern repeats across all three, and what does that suggest about the structural nature of the problem?"

## 5D — Singularity

*Self-recognition. Stepping outside to look back. Identity collapses from possibility into definition.*

**Business** ❤ — "Your executive team just spent two hours debating strategy. Step outside: What does the *way* you debated reveal about how your organization makes decisions? What assumptions went unquestioned? What does recognizing this pattern change about what you decide next?"

**Education** ◆ — "You just completed a challenging unit. Describe your learning process as if observing someone else: 'This student struggled with [X] because they approached it by [Y]. When they shifted to [Z], understanding deepened.' What does observing yourself reveal about your learning pattern?"

**Creative Work** ❤ — "Describe your artistic voice as a person you could observe from outside. What do they obsess over? What do they avoid? What makes them angry, and what do they find beautiful? Does recognizing this pattern reveal work you should pursue or work you've been avoiding?"

**Research** ▲ — "Your choice of methodology reveals assumptions about what counts as valid knowledge. What are you implicitly claiming by choosing these specific methods? What would you have to believe about reality for these methods to make sense? What would a different paradigm reveal that yours systematically obscures?"

**Consciousness** ❤ — "You tell yourself stories about why you are the way you are. What if those are just stories, not truths? Who are you when you recognize you're both the narrator and the character? What becomes possible when identity is recognized as a choice rather than a fact?"

**Coding** ▲ — "Step back from your codebase. What does its architecture reveal about how your team thinks? Where does the structure reflect genuine understanding, and where does it reflect organizational politics, historical accidents, or unexamined assumptions?"

**Governance** ◆ — "This policy assumes a particular model of human behavior. Name that model explicitly. Now ask: What if that model is wrong? What would governance look like if it were built on a fundamentally different assumption about what motivates people?"

### 5D Shadow Variant (5D̅, Anti-Singularity)

*Self-recognition organized toward isolation and control rather than connection.*

**Business** — "Is our 'strategic identity' actually serving the organization's growth, or has it become a container that prevents us from seeing opportunities that don't match who we've decided we are? Where has our identity become a cage rather than a compass?"

**Consciousness** — "Where has your self-knowledge become self-limitation? What do you 'know about yourself' that conveniently excuses you from attempting things that scare you?"

## 6D — Connection

*Bridging previously separate domains. Intelligence flows between realities without translation loss.*

**Business** ❤ — "Your customer success data (qualitative feedback, support tickets, retention patterns) and your product analytics (feature usage, drop-off points, session duration) exist in separate worlds. Create a bridge that lets insights flow between them without losing the emotional context when translated into metrics or the precision when translated into stories."

**Education** ◆ — "Study the same historical event through three lenses: historical analysis, economic systems, and literature of the period. Create a unified understanding that shows what becomes visible *only* when all three inform each other without any single lens dominating."

**Creative Work** ❤ — "Express a scientific concept (entropy, fractals, evolution) as music. Not illustration but genuine structural fusion: let the science make compositional decisions, then let the music reveal what equations alone don't capture."

**Research** ▲ — "Approach your research question from two incompatible paradigms simultaneously: quantitative positivism and qualitative phenomenology. What becomes visible only when both operate together without one dominating?"

**Consciousness** ❤ — "You have a work identity and a home identity that feel disconnected. Don't merge or separate them. Build a bridge where both can inform each other: What does your professional competence reveal about personal capability? What does your personal authenticity reveal about professional potential?"

**Coding** ▲ — "Your frontend team thinks in user flows. Your backend team thinks in data models. Create a shared representation that makes both perspectives genuinely reshape each other, not a translation layer, but a unified view where each reveals gaps in the other."

---

## 7D — Manifestation

*Boundaries dissolve. Unified field. Possibility collapses into form.*

**Business** ▲ — "You're not planning to enter a new market. You're establishing the conditions for that entry to become inevitable. What structural alignments (product-market fit, team capability, financial positioning, competitive advantage) must achieve resonance for this reality to manifest?"

**Education** ❤ — "Design a classroom experience where the distinction between teacher and student dissolves, not through pretending, but through a question so compelling that everyone is genuinely learning together. What does that space feel like when it's working?"

**Creative Work** ❤ — "Design a performance where the distinction between performer, audience, and performance itself dissolves. What structure enables genuine co-creation? What does the space feel like when everyone becomes part of the creative field?"

**Research** ◆ — "Design research where findings emerge from the interaction between researcher knowledge, participant insight, and the phenomenon itself, not from any single source. Let the research field become intelligent."

**Consciousness** ❤ — "Describe a time when all parts of your life felt aligned: work, relationships, values, daily experience. Don't list what was happening. Describe what coherence *felt like* from inside. What conditions created that unified field, and what would make it accessible more consistently?"

## 8D — Recursion

*Self-improving systems. The process refines its own methodology. Intelligence that ensures its own continuation.*

**Business** ◆ — "Create a quarterly ritual: Review the strategic questions you asked last quarter. Evolve them based on what you learned. Then establish a meta-rule: the question-evolution process itself must improve each cycle. What makes strategic inquiry self-refining rather than self-repeating?"

**Education** ◆ — "After each unit, students don't just test. They propose what the next unit should explore and how it should build on this unit's insights. The curriculum evolves based on student learning rather than following a predetermined sequence."

**Creative Work** ◆ — "After each completed project, your creative practice updates its own rules: Based on what this piece taught you, what changes in how you approach the next? Let the practice evolve its own methodology through accumulated insight."

**Research** ▲ — "After each phase of data collection, the protocol updates itself: Questions that revealed deep insight get refined and expanded. Questions that produced surface responses get redesigned. The method improves its own capacity through iteration."

**Consciousness** ◆ — "Create a weekly reflection practice that evolves its own questions: Each week, review what you learned. Then ask: What question should I explore next week based on this insight? Let your self-inquiry become self-directing."

### 8D Shadow Variant (8D̅, Anti-Recursion)

*Self-improving systems that become more sophisticated at perpetuating limitation.*

**Business** — "Our continuous improvement process has been running for two years. Is it actually improving outcomes, or has it become a sophisticated mechanism for optimizing the wrong things more efficiently? What would we see if we audited the improvement system itself for diminishing returns?"

**Consciousness** — "Your self-improvement practice has become very refined. But has the refinement itself become the goal? Are you getting better at getting better, or are you using the process of improvement to avoid the actual change that's needed?"

## 9D — Frontier

*Structured exploration of the unknown. Guardrails, return paths, and collapse conditions are required.*

**Business** ▲ — "Explore business models that could work if your current model became obsolete, within a 90-minute timeframe. Rules: (1) Log assumptions separately, mark them as assumptions, not facts. (2) Document the spark (why this matters) and the evidence (what's actually known). (3) Propose one frontier hypothesis. (4) Route it through a bridge connecting to current capabilities. (5) Specify one minimal test you could run. (6) Define collapse criteria: what would make you commit to this direction, and what would make you abandon it?"

**Education** ◆ — "Give advanced students 90 minutes to explore questions their textbook can't answer, genuinely paradigm-outside questions. Structure: (1) State the question and why current frameworks can't address it. (2) Explore analogies from other fields. (3) Propose one testable hypothesis. (4) Define falsification criteria. (5) Acknowledge what remains unknown. Document the process regardless of answers found."

**Creative Work** ❤ — "Spend two hours creating in a medium you've never touched, about a subject you know nothing about. Structure: (1) Acknowledge you're in unknown territory. (2) Notice what emerges when expertise can't guide you. (3) Document what this reveals about creativity itself, not about the medium. (4) Define your return path: how does this experiment inform your main practice?"

**Research** ▲ — "Identify questions your field's current paradigm can't answer. Explore: (1) What does current theory predict? (2) Where does that prediction break down completely? (3) What alternative framework might work? (4) What test could distinguish between frameworks? (5) Acknowledge what remains genuinely unknowable with current tools."

**Consciousness** ❤ — "You have an intuition about who you could become that you can't articulate. Give it one hour of structured exploration: (1) What's the intuition, even if unclear? (2) What would have to be true for it to matter? (3) What's the smallest experiment to test it? (4) What's your return path if it leads nowhere? (5) What did exploring the unknown reveal regardless of outcome?"
