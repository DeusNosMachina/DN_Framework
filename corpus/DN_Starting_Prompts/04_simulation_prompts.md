# Simulation Prompts

**Natural-language activation of the 5D Prompt Singularity simulation system**

---

These prompts translate the DN Kernel's formal simulation contracts (Sections 7.1–7.3) into facilitation-ready language. They are consistent with the 5D Prompt Singularity Configuration and can be used with or without a Growth Blueprint board.

**Principle:** Diagnose before you intervene. Chain commands for compound intelligence. Every output passes through the Pillar Metric.

---

## Diagnostic Commands

*Always start here when the field is unclear.*

### Run Dimensional Audit

*What dimensions are active? What's missing? Where's the shadow?*

> Run a Dimensional Audit on [scope: full project / specific area / this conversation so far]. Show me the dimensional distribution: where is intelligence concentrated, and where are the gaps? Report shadow presence or absence at each dimension. If any dimension shows weak creative presence, check whether strong shadow presence exists there before concluding the dimension is underdeveloped. Capability organized toward limitation looks different from capability that's absent. Flag any dimensional blindspots.

**When to use:** First diagnostic step. When things feel unclear, scattered, or when you suspect the work is concentrated in too few dimensions.

### Initiate Gravity Mapping

*Where does attention actually concentrate? Does it match stated priorities?*

> Map the gravity of [scope]: where are resources, attention, and energy actually concentrating? Identify which elements exert the strongest pull. Compare where gravity *is* with where it *should be* given our stated priorities. If there's a mismatch, if strategic goals are starved while lower-priority areas attract disproportionate attention, name it. Suggest resource allocation for optimal impact.

**When to use:** Pairs with Dimensional Audit as the standard opening. When the board feels busy but not productive, or when stated priorities don't match observed effort.

### Map Tension Field

*Where are the opposing forces? Is the tension creative or destructive?*

**Scan Mode:**
> Scan [scope] for structural tension, places where ideas, strategies, or perspectives assert incompatible claims. Identify tension pairs: what opposes what, at which dimension, and with what intensity. Classify each as creative (generating new insight through opposition), destructive (calcified entrenchment blocking movement), or unresolved (not yet engaged).

**Diagnostic Mode:**
> Map the tension field across [scope] at full diagnostic depth. For each tension pair: identify the pillar divergence (are they disagreeing through Heart, Truth, or Nuance?), the dimensional position where the tension manifests, and whether synthesis is possible at a higher dimension. Surface any tension pairs where opposition appears resolvable, for example two competing 3D contexts that a 5D identity could transcend, or two competing strategies that a 6D bridge could hold.

**Narrative Mode** (for opposing 5D+ positions):
> We have two competing identities/visions: [Position A] and [Position B]. Don't resolve this. Analyze the space *between* them: What does Position A illuminate that B cannot see? What does Position B illuminate that A misses? Is there a higher-dimensional synthesis that could encompass both without forcing compromise? What intelligence lives in the gap between these positions that neither has access to alone?

**When to use:** When competing claims coexist without resolution. When coherence is declining. When two strong perspectives are entrenched. *Always diagnose tension before attempting to bridge it.*

## Primary Strategic Commands

### Ignite Reality Rift

*What if? Explore diverging futures.*

> Ignite Reality Rift for [subject/strategy/decision]. Generate [2-4] scenarios exploring what happens if we pursue different paths from our current position. For each scenario: narrate what unfolds, identify which existing elements are affected, track dimensional shifts, assess risk, and surface which shadow exposures each path creates. Evaluate both creative and shadow orientations of each scenario. Tag which pillar leads each scenario's analysis.

**When to use:** High-uncertainty strategic forks. When the question is *which path*, not *what's wrong*.

### Forge Love Bridge

*Connect what seems separate. Surface hidden resonance.*

> Forge Love Bridge between [Element A] and [Element B]. These seem disconnected but shouldn't be. Find the resonance points, the structural reasons these elements belong in relationship. For each connection found: explain why it exists, score its resonance strength, map the dimensional pathway between them, and identify which pillar leads the connection. Show what becomes visible only when these elements are understood as related.

**When to use:** When the field feels fragmented. When insights from different areas need to inform each other. Not for imbalance; if things feel lopsided, audit first.

### Usher Golden Age

*Project the ideal future and map the path there.*

**Vision Mode:**
> Usher Golden Age for [subject/scope]. Paint the picture of what fully realized success looks and feels like. Not a plan, but the *state* we're moving toward. What does daily reality look like when this vision is manifest? What does it feel like to operate inside it?

**Milestone Mode:**
> Usher Golden Age for [subject/scope] in Milestone Mode over [time horizon]. From the current state, map the transition chain: what specific dimensional shifts must occur, in what order, to reach the goal state? Each milestone should identify the dimension it operates at, the transition mechanism required, the evidence that would confirm completion, and which pillar leads the movement.

**When to use:** When the goal state is known but the path isn't. Vision Mode for inspiration and alignment. Milestone Mode for actionable transition chains.

### Weave Dimensional Thread

*The full symphony: trace an idea through all nine dimensions.*

> Weave a Dimensional Thread for [subject/project/brand]. Trace its story from 1D Spark through 9D Frontier, one beat per dimension. At each beat: narrate what happens, reference relevant existing elements where they ground the story, note the shadow expression, and tag which pillar leads. Identify the 5D Singularity Point, the moment of defining recognition, and any Signal Lock candidates where all three pillars converge.

**When to use:** Long-form thinking. Leadership alignment. Investor narratives. When you need to see the whole arc.

## Depth Commands

### Spark Fire Test

*Stress-test under pressure. What survives?*

> Spark Fire Test on [scope/strategy/idea] against [test mode: Market Shock / Team Friction / Scaling Pressure] at [intensity: standard / extreme]. For each element tested: did it survive, strengthen, or collapse? For anything that broke, what was the fracture point and what refinement path could strengthen it? Produce a fragility map showing vulnerability distribution across the scope. Evaluate both creative and shadow orientations under stress.

**When to use:** When assumptions feel untested. When something seems too good. Before committing significant resources. Pairs naturally with Shadow Symmetry as a follow-up.

### Explore Shadow Symmetry

*Surface what the creative layer doesn't articulate.*

**Singular Mode:**
> Explore Shadow Symmetry for [scope/elements]. Generate the most probable shadow counterpart for each creative-layer element, not the opposite, but the parallel structure where the same capability organizes toward limitation. Link each shadow signal back to its creative source. What risk, blindspot, or destructive recursion pattern does each shadow reveal?

**Scenario Mode:**
> Explore Shadow Symmetry for [scope/elements] in Scenario mode, [2-3] scenarios each. For each element, generate distinct shadow scenarios exploring different risk vectors. Label each scenario clearly. What does the shadow landscape look like when all scenarios are viewed together?

**When to use:** After Fire Test to deepen what stress revealed. When creative work feels suspiciously clean. When blindspots are suspected.

### Reveal Resonance Field

*After a defining recognition, re-read everything through the new lens.*

> A 5D+ recognition just occurred: [describe the recognition, such as Signal Lock, identity definition, or strategic commitment]. Reveal Resonance Field: which existing elements now carry *elevated* significance (they matter more than before), *diminished* significance (they matter less), or have been *recontextualized* (same data, fundamentally different meaning)? Don't move anything. Evaluate meaning-shift-in-place. What does the field look like when re-read through this new recognition?

**When to use:** Immediately after a Signal Lock or identity commitment. The significance shift is clearest right away.

## Universal Commands

### Generate Resonance Result

*What actually shifted?*

> Generate Resonance Result for [the simulation just completed]. What dimensional insight emerged most clearly? Did any element achieve convergence across all three pillars (Signal Lock candidate)? What cross-links to other areas became visible? What's the one thing that changed in our understanding?

**When to use:** After any simulation. The universal close.

### Create Storyfield

*Narrate the journey of becoming.*

> Create Storyfield for [entity: project, product, brand, organization, person]. Map its dimensional journey: What was the spark (1D)? What tested it (2D)? What system did it enter (3D)? What patterns did time reveal (4D)? What identity crystallized (5D)? What bridges formed (6D)? What manifested (7D)? What recursion sustains it (8D)? What frontier calls (9D)? Identify the Singularity Point, any missed bridges, and which dimension still echoes, the unresolved energy that persists most strongly.

**When to use:** When you need to understand an entity's dimensional arc. For leadership narratives, brand stories, or understanding how thinking evolved.

## Proven Sequences

| Sequence | Commands | Use When |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard Opening** | Audit → Gravity Map | Entering unfamiliar territory or starting a new session |
| **Stress Sequence** | Fire Test → Shadow Symmetry | Testing resilience then exploring what fragility reveals |
| **Vision Sequence** | Reality Rift → Dimensional Thread | Exploring futures then weaving the chosen path into narrative |
| **Projection Sequence** | Golden Age → Storyfield | Projecting ideal future then narrating how to arrive there |
| **Tension Sequence** | Tension Map → Love Bridge | Diagnosing polarity then attempting connection. Name before bridging. |
| **Recognition Sequence** | Tension Map (Narrative) → Resonance Field | Understanding opposing identities then re-reading the field through each |
| **Universal Close** | Any command → Resonance Result | Always. Wrap every simulation session. |
