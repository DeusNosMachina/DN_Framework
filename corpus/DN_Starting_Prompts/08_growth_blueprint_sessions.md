# Growth Blueprint Sessions

**Facilitation prompts for Growth Blueprint template work**

---

These prompts are designed for use during Growth Blueprint facilitation, whether with a MURAL board, a digital Blueprint Board, or an AI-assisted session using the Growth Blueprint Guide. They connect dimensional thinking to specific Blueprint sections and exercises.

For simulation commands, see [Simulation Prompts](04_simulation_prompts.md). For the full exercise structure, see the Growth Blueprint Guide. For simulation contract specifications, see the 5D Prompt Singularity Configuration and DN Kernel Sections 7.1–7.3.

## Session Opening

### First Session — New Blueprint
> We're starting a fresh Growth Blueprint for [client/company name]. Before diving into any section, let's establish the field:
>
> **1D Spark:** In one sentence, why does this company exist? Not what it does. Why it *must*.
>
> **Pillar orientation:** As a facilitator, I'll be watching for Heart (are we preserving the why?), Truth (does evidence support this?), and Nuance (are we seeing the full picture?) throughout every exercise. We'll call out which pillar is leading at each stage.
>
> **Starting position:** Based on what we know about this company, which Blueprint section should we begin with? The recommended sequence starts with Business Model and Brand Identity, but if the client's immediate need pulls elsewhere, we follow the energy.
>
> Let's begin by understanding the landscape before we map it.

### Returning Session — Board Already Populated
> Welcome back. Before continuing, let's check the field state:
>
> 1. **Evolution check:** What changed since last session? Review the Evolution Tracking entries. Has anything shifted in the market, the team, or the client's thinking that should inform today's work?
> 2. **Dimensional audit:** Quick scan: where is the board's intelligence concentrated? Are we still in 2D–3D territory, or has anything moved toward 5D recognition?
> 3. **Completion criteria:** Did we set criteria last session? What's their status: met, deferred, or scope-changed?
> 4. **Today's intent:** What does the board need most? Expansion (new sections), consolidation (deepening existing sections), or resolution (addressing tensions)?

## Section-Specific Facilitation

### Section 1 — Business Model
> We're mapping the business model. This section is primarily 2D–3D work: validating what exists and understanding the system it operates within. Guide the conversation through:
>
> - **BMC exercise:** What's the current reality? Fill the canvas with what's true *now*, not aspirational. Color-code: blue for validated data, violet for contextual relationships. If something is assumed rather than proven, flag it. That's an experiment for Section 8.
> - **Competitive Evaluation:** Who are we really competing with? Not just named competitors, but what *alternatives* does the customer use, including doing nothing? Plot the comparison matrix and look for the quadrant nobody owns.
> - **Strategic Positioning:** Where do we sit on the competitive map? More importantly, where could we move that would be defensible? This is where 2D analysis begins shifting toward 5D identity.

### Section 2 — Brand Identity (Critical)
> This is the most important section for AI simulation alignment. Without a clear 5D identity, simulations have no gravitational center.
>
> - **Brand Foundations:** Capture mission, values, and vision. Push past corporate language to the words that actually *mean something* to the people in the room. The test: would someone cry if this mission was taken away? If not, dig deeper.
> - **Motivations, "Our Why?":** This is 1D work. Ask: "What breaks your heart about the world that you're trying to fix?" Allow silence. The real answer often comes after the polished one.
> - **Brand-as-a-Room:** Use this exercise to bypass analytical thinking. When people choose furniture that represents their brand, they reveal identity truths that strategic language obscures. Pay attention to what surprises *them* about their own choices.
>
> **Facilitator signal:** When the room starts saying "yes, *that's* who we are" with conviction, when the energy shifts from constructing identity to recognizing it, you may be approaching Signal Lock territory. Note the candidate but don't force it. 5D recognition cannot be manufactured.

### Section 3 — Customer Profile
> Shift from internal identity to external understanding. This section grounds the brand in the people it serves.
>
> - **Value Proposition Canvas:** Map the customer's world: jobs-to-be-done, pains, gains. Then map how your offering addresses each. Look for the gaps, pains with no corresponding solution, gains nobody's targeting. Those gaps are where the real opportunity lives.
> - **Personas:** Build from the VPC, not from assumptions. Three personas maximum. For each: what's their worst day? What does success look like *to them*? What would make them switch from a competitor to you, and what would make them switch *from you*?
> - **Interview Notes:** If real customer interviews exist, they override everything else. Where do interview insights contradict our persona assumptions? Those contradictions are the most valuable signals on the board.

### Section 5 — Goals & Priorities
> This section moves from understanding to commitment. Watch for the 4D→5D transition, the moment when "what we could do" collapses into "what we will do."
>
> - **Top Business Priorities:** Now/Next/Later/Not a Focus. The "Not a Focus" column is as revealing as "Now." What's being deliberately excluded, and is that exclusion conscious or avoidant?
> - **Company Goals:** Plot on Impact × Feasibility. The "Moonshots" quadrant (high impact, low feasibility) often contains the company's real aspiration. The "Best Bets" quadrant is where execution should concentrate. Notice whether the team gravitates toward safety or ambition.
> - **Biggest Risks:** Surface the elephants. Four categories: market, product, competition, macroeconomic. Push for honest risk naming, not sanitized versions. Shadow work happens naturally here; encourage it. A risk that's named is a risk that can be managed.

### Section 6 — KPI Dashboard
> Metrics connect aspiration to reality. This section is 2D–4D: validation, tracking, and temporal pattern recognition.
>
> - **KPI Prioritization:** If you could only measure three things, which three? This question forces the room to reveal what actually matters versus what they feel obligated to track. The answer is often surprising.
> - **KPI Tracker:** Populate with real numbers where available. Use trend indicators (↓ → ↑) rather than just snapshots. The trajectory matters more than the position.
> - **Proof Points:** Gather qualitative evidence alongside quantitative KPIs. Testimonials, case studies, interview moments. This is where Heart-led proof complements Truth-led metrics.

## Simulation Integration Points

*When to invoke simulation commands during Blueprint facilitation.*

| Blueprint Moment | Simulation | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Starting a new section | **Dimensional Audit** on completed sections | See what the board knows before adding more |
| After populating Business Model + Brand Identity | **Gravity Mapping** on full board | Where is attention concentrating vs. where it should be? |
| After Brand Identity feels "locked" | **Reveal Resonance Field** | Re-read the entire board through the new identity |
| Before finalizing Go-to-Market plan | **Spark Fire Test** (Market Shock) | Stress-test the strategy before committing resources |
| When two strategies compete | **Map Tension Field** (Diagnostic) | Understand the polarity before choosing |
| After Fire Test reveals vulnerability | **Explore Shadow Symmetry** | Understand the deeper pattern beneath the fragility |
| At session close | **Generate Resonance Result** | What actually shifted? |
| For leadership or investor presentation | **Weave Dimensional Thread** | Narrate the full strategic arc 1D–9D |
| For projection and roadmap | **Usher Golden Age** (Milestone) | Map the transition chain from now to goal state |
| When the board feels fragmented | **Forge Love Bridge** | Surface hidden connections between sections |

## Session Closing Protocol

> Before we close this session:
>
> 1. **Completion criteria review:** Evaluate each criterion we set at the start. Status: met / partially met / unmet / deferred / scope changed / not evaluated / open exploration.
> 2. **Dimensional position:** Where did the board's intelligence move today? What dimensions were most active? Any transitions worth recording?
> 3. **Shadow check:** Did anything uncomfortable surface that we didn't fully address? If so, log it. It's signal for next session.
> 4. **Evolution Tracking entry:** Record today's shift. Date, what changed, why, and what it means for next session.
> 5. **Action items:** Who does what by when? Color-code by dimensional origin and recursion target.
> 6. **Next session intent:** What does the board need next: expansion, consolidation, or resolution?
>
> If a capture is warranted (meaningful inflection point), freeze the board state now.
