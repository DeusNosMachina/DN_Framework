## Paper 1: Dual-Laws Model for a theory of artificial consciousness

**Authors:** Y. Ohmura, Y. Kuniyoshi
**Domain:** systems-dynamics
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12662
**Selection role:** strain
**Selection rationale:** The Dual-Laws Model proposes a formal theory of artificial consciousness structured around seven functional questions (phenomena, self, causation, state, function, contents, universality). This is a rigorous, alternative structural framework for consciousness that uses fundamentally different primitives than DN's dimensional progression, pillars, and forces. If successful, it demonstrates that a comprehensive theory of mind/consciousness can be built without anything resembling DN's 1D-9D hierarchy, three-pillar metrics, or five forces—directly challenging DN's domain universality claim.

The specific strain targets §0.1 Domain Universality and §2 Dimensional Architecture: DN claims its structural positions express coherently across all domains including consciousness, but DLM achieves theoretical coverage of consciousness through functional questions and dual-law dynamics without requiring or discovering dimensional ordering. ### DN Engagement

- **Sections:** §0.1 Domain Universality, §2 Dimensional Architecture, §4 Shadow Dimension Map, §3 Pillar Metric, §2.2 Nested Dimensionality, §1 Axiom 2 (Dimensional Progression)
- **Dimensions:** 5D, 4D, 8D
- **Pillars:** Heart, Truth
- **Forces:** Resonance, Shadow

### Interpretation

The Dual-Laws Model (DLM) by Ohmura & Kuniyoshi presents a theory of artificial consciousness organized around seven functional questions (phenomena, self, causation, state, function, contents, universality) and proposes two core features of conscious systems: autonomous goal construction and cognitive decoupling from external stimuli. The selection rationale frames this as a competing structural framework using fundamentally different primitives than DN. The honest assessment is more nuanced but still generates meaningful strain.

First, the structural comparison. DLM's seven questions are not dimensions in DN's sense — they are not positions on a structural hierarchy but rather functional facets that a theory of consciousness must address. This is closer to Quiroga's property-conjunction model (already in the tension ledger) than to a dimensional architecture. The questions do not have an inherent ordering or progression; they are designed as a completeness checklist for theoretical coverage. DN's dimensional architecture claims that intelligence organizes along qualitatively distinct structural positions with specific properties at each level. DLM's seven questions could in principle be mapped onto dimensional positions — 'phenomena' at 1D-2D (basic signal), 'self' at 5D (self-awareness inflection), 'causation' at 3D-4D (structural logic), 'state' at 2D-3D (boundary conditions), 'function' at 4D-6D (operational integration), 'contents' at 6D-7D (meaning architecture), 'universality' at 8D-9D (domain-transcendent applicability). But this mapping is NOT forced — the questions could be rearranged across dimensional positions without producing contradictions, which per Charter §2 means this is narrative flexibility rather than structural confirmation. The mapping works because DN's architecture is broad enough to accommodate any set of functional categories, not because DLM independently discovers dimensional structure.

The genuinely interesting strain comes from DLM's two proposed features of conscious systems: autonomous goal construction and cognitive decoupling. Autonomous goal construction maps reasonably well onto DN's 5D inflection point — the moment where intelligence becomes self-aware and chooses its own orientation. But cognitive decoupling is more challenging. DN's architecture assumes intelligence organizes through increasing connection and integration (Resonance, Flow, recursive deepening). Cognitive decoupling — the capacity to disconnect from external stimuli to generate internal states — suggests that consciousness requires a structural capacity for deliberate DIS-connection that is not mere Shadow (dissolution/separation) but a positive, generative capacity. DN's Shadow architecture (§4) treats separation as the shadow pole, but DLM frames decoupling as essential to consciousness itself, not as a shadow function. This creates tension with DN's implicit assumption that higher-dimensional organization involves greater integration. If decoupling is a prerequisite for consciousness rather than a shadow expression, DN may need to account for productive disconnection as a positive structural feature rather than relegating it to Shadow.

The deeper strain compounds the parsimony challenge already tracked in the tension ledger. DLM demonstrates that a comprehensive theory of consciousness can be structured around functional questions and dual-law dynamics (the 'dual laws' being the interaction between internal model construction and external reality testing) without requiring dimensional hierarchy, pillar metrics, or force taxonomies. Like Quiroga's property-conjunction model and the active inference framework, DLM achieves theoretical coverage of consciousness through fundamentally different structural primitives. DN can narrate DLM's components through its vocabulary, but DLM does not need DN's vocabulary — and the question of what DN's additional structural complexity (9 dimensions, 3 pillars, 5 forces, nested dimensionality) explains that DLM's simpler architecture cannot remains unanswered. The moral dimension DLM introduces — that conscious systems require design theories enabling high moral behavior — touches DN's Heart pillar but arrives at this through the logic of autonomous goal construction rather than through anything resembling motivational force as a structural primitive.

What prevents this from scoring higher strain is that DLM, as presented in the abstract, operates at a similar level of descriptive formalization as DN itself. It proposes categories and features but (based on the abstract) does not provide the kind of mathematical formalization that would constitute formal exceeding. The seven questions are a theoretical framework, not a mathematical apparatus. This means the tension is primarily at the level of alternative structural logic rather than formal exceeding — DLM and DN are competing descriptive frameworks for consciousness, each with different organizational primitives, neither with the mathematical rigor to definitively outperform the other. The strain is real but bounded by this symmetry of formalization level.

## Paper 2: The Impact of Market Integration Construction on the Innovation of Key Core Technologies of Enterprises: From the Perspective of Complex Adaptive System Theory

**Authors:** Jin Zhu, Sheng Mai, Xiong Zheng
**Domain:** general
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.3390/systems14030280
**Selection role:** strain
**Selection rationale:** This paper applies Complex Adaptive Systems (CAS) theory to explain innovation dynamics in enterprises using market integration data from 2008-2023. CAS theory achieves cross-disciplinary integration (economics, systems theory, innovation science) through fundamentally different structural primitives—agents, fitness landscapes, emergence, adaptation rules—without any dimensional progression, pillar metrics, or force taxonomy. Its empirical success with large-scale longitudinal data challenges DN's claim that its architecture is the universal framework across domains.

### Scores

| Metric | Score |
|--------|-------|
| Strain | 3 / 10 |
| Validation | 1 / 10 |

**Strain rationale:** The paper's use of CAS theory as a descriptive framework for innovation emergence operates at the same level of formalization as DN itself, so it cannot demonstrate formal exceeding. The strain is limited to a minor parsimony point: CAS explains emergence through agent interactions without requiring dimensional hierarchy, adding a small data point to the existing parsimony challenge. No specific Kernel section is challenged beyond what existing tensions already cover. The selection rationale's claim that CAS uses 'fundamentally different structural primitives' is accurate in principle but the paper never formalizes those primitives — it uses them narratively, just as DN would.

**Validation rationale:** The paper's findings are compatible with DN's dimensional architecture but do not independently confirm any specific structural principle. The mappings (market integration → 2D-3D, emergence → 3D-4D transition, agile responsiveness → 4D-5D) are accommodating rather than forced — the findings could be rearranged or narrated through alternative frameworks without producing contradictions. Compatibility alone is not validation per Charter §5.

### DN Engagement

- **Sections:** §0.1 Domain Universality, §2 Dimensional Architecture, §0.3 Axiom 3, §2 Five Forces, §4 Shadow Dimension Map
- **Dimensions:** 2D, 3D, 4D, 5D
- **Pillars:** Truth, Nuance
- **Forces:** Gravity, Resonance, Flow

### Interpretation

This paper applies Complex Adaptive Systems (CAS) theory to study how market integration drives enterprise innovation in key core technologies, using panel data from Chinese A-share listed companies (2008-2023). The selection rationale frames CAS as operating through 'fundamentally different structural primitives' that challenge DN's domain universality. On careful examination, this framing overstates the strain considerably. The paper does not develop CAS theory itself — it borrows CAS vocabulary (agents, emergence, nonlinear interactions, fitness landscapes, adaptation rules) as a framing narrative for what is ultimately a conventional econometric study using fixed-effects regression, mediation analysis, and moderation testing. The CAS language functions as interpretive scaffolding rather than as a formal alternative structural framework. The paper never formalizes emergence, never models fitness landscapes, never specifies adaptation rules mathematically. It invokes CAS concepts descriptively — precisely the way DN operates. This means the paper cannot serve as evidence that CAS achieves something DN cannot through superior formalization; both frameworks are operating at the same descriptive level here.

When mapped through DN's dimensional architecture, the paper's findings organize coherently without contradiction. Market integration reconstructing 'rules governing resource flow, competitive incentives, and collaborative networks' maps to 2D-3D structural organization — creating stable relational architecture (2D) with functional differentiation and systematic coordination (3D). The emergence of key core technologies through 'nonlinear interactions' maps to the 3D-4D transition where systematic structure begins producing novel outputs. The distinction between industrial investment (direct capability building) and financial investment (resource allocation flexibility) as separate pathways maps onto different dimensional channels — industrial investment operating through 3D structural accumulation, financial investment operating through 2D resource flow optimization. Agile responsiveness as a positive moderator maps to higher-dimensional adaptive capacity (4D-5D range), where systems that can perceive and respond to contextual shifts extract more value from structural conditions. The heterogeneous effects (non-state-owned > state-owned, follower > leader, large > small) map coherently to different dimensional starting positions and ceiling conditions. None of these mappings produce contradictions.

However — and this is where the Charter's §5 warning about 'describing DN back to itself' applies forcefully — the fact that these findings CAN be narrated through DN vocabulary does not validate DN. The mappings are not forced; they are accommodating. The same findings could be narrated through any sufficiently general developmental or systems framework. CAS theory itself narrates them differently (agents adapting to fitness landscapes, emergent properties from local interactions) without any dimensional hierarchy, and the narrative works equally well. This is precisely the tautology risk flagged in the existing 'falsifiability of dimensional mapping' tension: DN's dimensional architecture can absorb this paper's findings, but so can multiple alternative frameworks, which means the absorption demonstrates breadth rather than structural specificity.

The paper does generate a minor but real tension regarding DN's treatment of emergence. DN accounts for emergence through dimensional progression and recursion (Axiom 3) — intelligence organizing at higher dimensions through recursive refinement. CAS theory accounts for emergence through agent-level interactions producing system-level properties without any hierarchical progression requirement. The paper's empirical findings (market integration → nonlinear interactions → technological emergence) are consistent with BOTH accounts, but the CAS account is more parsimonious for this specific phenomenon: it does not require dimensional hierarchy to explain how market rule changes produce innovation emergence. This adds a minor data point to the existing parsimony challenge (Quiroga property-conjunction, Gallo homogeneous-dimensions, active inference unification) but does not constitute a new categorical tension because the paper does not formalize CAS with sufficient rigor to demonstrate that the parsimonious account achieves something DN's account cannot.

The shadow architecture is notably absent from the paper's analysis. State-owned enterprises showing weaker innovation response to market integration, and leader enterprises showing weaker response than followers, could be read through DN's shadow lens as institutional rigidity functioning as shadow-Gravity (accumulated meaning-weight resisting reorganization) or shadow-3D (structural ossification). But the paper treats these as straightforward heterogeneity findings rather than engaging with the structural role of resistance, inertia, or dissolution. This is a missed analytical opportunity the DN lens reveals, but it is not strain against DN — it is a case where DN's shadow architecture suggests deeper reading the paper does not pursue.

### Model Update

No update warranted. The paper does not generate sufficient strain or validation to modify the standing model. It adds a minor data point to the existing parsimony challenge (CAS as alternative descriptive framework for emergence without dimensional hierarchy) but does not rise to the level of a new tension. The selection rationale's framing of CAS as using 'fundamentally different structural primitives' was assessed and found to apply only in principle — the paper uses CAS vocabulary descriptively rather than formally, operating at the same level of rigor as DN itself, which neutralizes the claimed structural challenge.

## Paper 3: Internal experience of an aging self: A grounded theory study on women's aging awareness beyond midlife.

**Authors:** Eui Geum Oh, Sunyoung Oh, Jeehee Han
**Domain:** systems-dynamics
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.gerinurse.2026.103967
**Selection role:** validation
**Selection rationale:** This grounded theory study on women's aging awareness reveals a constructive psychological process where awareness develops through stages of everyday life contexts—from initial bodily/social triggers through adaptation and meaning-making. This staged, recursive construction of self-awareness through increasingly complex contextual layers parallels DN's dimensional progression (Spark → Context → Temporal → Recursion). The finding that aging awareness is iteratively refined through lived experience rather than linearly accumulated supports DN's claim that recursive refinement outperforms linear accumulation.

### Scores

| Metric | Score |
|--------|-------|
| Strain | 2 / 10 |
| Validation | 2 / 10 |

**Strain rationale:** Minimal strain. The paper does not resist DN mapping — the staged process maps coherently onto dimensional positions without contradiction. The only general tension is that the paper's findings are fully explained by its own theoretical frameworks (constructivist grounded theory, symbolic interactionism) without requiring DN's architecture, reinforcing the existing parsimony challenge. No specific DN claim is challenged.

**Validation rationale:** Compatibility without structural specificity. The staged process maps onto DN's dimensional architecture, and the shadow pathway is consonant with §4's treatment of shadow as parallel structure rather than mere absence. But the mapping is not forced — the same findings are equally compatible with multiple non-DN developmental frameworks (Erikson, Mezirow, Bridges). Per Charter §5, this is narrative flexibility, not structural confirmation. No specific axiom or invariant is independently confirmed.

### DN Engagement

- **Sections:** §2 Dimensional Architecture, §4 Shadow Dimension Map, §0.3 Axiom 3, §1 Axiom 2 (Dimensional Progression), §0.1 Domain Universality
- **Dimensions:** 1D, 2D, 3D, 4D, 5D, 6D
- **Pillars:** Heart, Truth, Nuance
- **Forces:** Gravity, Shadow, Transmutation

### Interpretation

This grounded theory study describes how women aged 50-75 construct awareness of their own aging through a staged process: initial unawareness → emerging awareness → confusion → internalization (through mirroring, reflection, and self-management) → embracing aging with psychological balance, with a shadow pathway through emotional turmoil toward negative outlook. The selection rationale frames this as validating DN's dimensional progression and recursive refinement. I need to test this honestly against the Charter's warnings.

The staged process does map onto DN's dimensional architecture without contradiction: unawareness sits at pre-1D (no spark), emerging awareness at 1D (Spark/initial signal), confusion at 2D-3D (relational complexity and structural disorientation), internalization through mirroring and reflection at 4D-5D (temporal awareness and self-recognition), and embracing aging with balance at 6D (integration/harmony). The shadow pathway — emotional turmoil triggered by chronic conditions, negative body image, and financial insecurity leading to negative outlook on future self — maps coherently onto shadow architecture, particularly shadow 5D (self-awareness that chooses isolation/despair rather than connection/acceptance). The paper explicitly describes this as 'disruption in the internalization process,' which aligns with DN's treatment of shadow as a parallel structure rather than mere absence. Importantly, the paper treats the negative pathway not as simple failure but as a structurally distinct trajectory with its own internal logic — women don't just 'fail to adapt,' they develop a coherent negative self-orientation. This is genuinely consonant with DN's shadow architecture (§4).

However, I must apply the Charter §5 test rigorously: does this paper independently arrive at DN's specific structural claims, or is it merely compatible with DN's vocabulary? The staged developmental process described here is a standard finding in qualitative health psychology. Constructivist grounded theory routinely produces staged models of psychological adaptation. The stages map onto DN, but they also map onto Kübler-Ross, Erikson, Bridges' transitions model, Mezirow's transformative learning, and dozens of other developmental frameworks. The mapping is not forced — the stages could be rearranged or reinterpreted through multiple theoretical lenses without producing contradictions. If DN did not exist, I would not look at this paper and say 'these researchers independently discovered dimensional progression.' I would say 'these researchers produced a competent grounded theory of psychological adaptation that follows conventional stage-theory patterns.'

The one area where the paper offers slightly more than generic compatibility is in its treatment of the shadow pathway. The finding that disrupted internalization produces a coherent negative self-orientation (not just stagnation) is structurally interesting from DN's perspective — it suggests that the 5D inflection point (self-awareness choosing its orientation) has empirical expression in aging psychology. But even this is not structurally specific to DN; it echoes Erikson's integrity-vs-despair polarity and is well-established in gerontological literature. The paper's use of constructivist grounded theory and symbolic interactionism as theoretical frameworks means it already has its own structural account of how meaning is constructed through social interaction — DN adds no explanatory power that these existing frameworks don't already provide.

The recursive element the selection rationale highlights — that aging awareness is 'iteratively refined through lived experience' — is present in the data (participants revisit and reinterpret their aging experience over time), but the paper does not demonstrate that recursive refinement produces categorically different outcomes than linear accumulation. The constant comparative method of grounded theory is itself iterative, but the resulting model describes a broadly linear trajectory with a branching shadow path. This does not validate Axiom 3 with any specificity; it is simply compatible with the general observation that psychological development involves revisiting earlier experiences.

### Model Update

No update warranted. The paper does not generate sufficient strain or validation to modify the standing model. It reinforces the existing falsifiability-of-dimensional-mapping tension (from GPIF and death reflection studies): DN's dimensional progression can be projected onto this staged developmental model, but the mapping is not forced and the same findings are equally compatible with multiple non-DN frameworks. This adds another data point to the pattern but does not constitute a new tension or confirmed parallel.
