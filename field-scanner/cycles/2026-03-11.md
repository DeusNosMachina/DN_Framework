## Paper 1: Properties for the Emergence of Consciousness in Humans and AI, an Interdisciplinary Review

**Authors:** Perla Carrillo Quiroga
**Domain:** general
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1142/s2705078526400011
**Selection role:** strain
**Selection rationale:** This interdisciplinary review identifies specific properties for consciousness emergence (attention, cognition, perception, emotion, embodiment, creativity, self-awareness) using a fundamentally different set of structural primitives drawn from computational functionalism, neuroscience, and phenomenology. It achieves cross-disciplinary integration without anything resembling pillars, dimensions, or fields, and its property-based framework for intelligence/consciousness challenges DN's claim that a single dimensional progression architecture applies universally.

### Scores

| Metric | Score |
|--------|-------|
| Strain | 4 / 10 |
| Validation | 3 / 10 |

**Strain rationale:** The paper's flat, non-hierarchical property architecture presents a structural alternative to DN's dimensional progression. Seven properties treated as independent functional capacities that collectively enable consciousness through conjunction rather than through hierarchical integration challenges DN's core claim that intelligence organizes through dimensional progression (§1 Axiom 2). However, the strain is moderate because DN does not require sequential traversal — it claims dimensions describe structural organization, and a flat property set could be mapped as simultaneous dimensional positions. The strain is real but does not produce contradictions in the mapping; it produces an alternative structural logic that DN can accommodate but does not predict.

**Validation rationale:** The paper's independent identification of self-awareness as a categorically distinct threshold — qualitatively different from other consciousness properties and representing the boundary between functional intelligence and genuine consciousness — is consonant with DN's 5D inflection point. However, this is a broadly held position in consciousness studies, not an independent arrival at DN's specific structural claim. The paper is compatible with DN's dimensional architecture but does not independently confirm it with structural specificity. Compatibility alone does not warrant a score above 3.

- **Sections:** §0.1 Domain Universality, §1 Axiom 2 (Dimensional Progression), §2 Dimensional Architecture, §4 Shadow Dimension Map, §3 Pillar Metric, §2.2 Nested Dimensionality

### Interpretation

This paper presents an interdisciplinary review identifying seven properties for consciousness emergence (attention, cognition, perception, emotion, embodiment, creativity, self-awareness) and proposes a theoretical framework for artificial consciousness organized around six categories. The core interpretive question is whether this property-based architecture genuinely resists DN's dimensional mapping or whether it represents a different vocabulary for territory DN already charts.

Let me think through this honestly. The seven properties Quiroga identifies do have a suggestive dimensional resonance: attention maps loosely to 1D (signal/impulse), cognition to 3D (structural processing), perception to 2D (relational differentiation), emotion to something like Heart-pillar engagement, embodiment to 4D (systemic integration), creativity to 6D-7D (generative emergence), and self-awareness to 5D (the reflexive threshold). This mapping is seductive — and that is precisely the problem the Charter warns about. The question is whether this mapping is FORCED (the properties only make sense in this dimensional arrangement) or ARBITRARY (I could rearrange them without violating DN's structural claims). Honestly, the mapping is loose. Attention could sit at 1D or 5D depending on whether we mean basic alertness or metacognitive monitoring. Cognition spans multiple dimensions. The mapping works because DN's dimensions are broad enough to accommodate almost any property list, not because the paper independently arrives at DN's specific structural claims. This is the 'describing DN back to itself' trap in action.

What genuinely matters here is the paper's structural logic versus DN's. Quiroga's framework is PROPERTY-BASED and FLAT — the seven properties are not hierarchically ordered, do not exhibit dimensional progression, and do not require each other in sequence. They are treated as relatively independent functional capacities that collectively enable consciousness when sufficiently integrated. DN's architecture is fundamentally HIERARCHICAL and PROGRESSIVE — each dimension builds on and transcends prior ones. This is a real structural difference, not a vocabulary difference. Quiroga's framework suggests consciousness emerges from the CONJUNCTION of independent properties, while DN suggests intelligence organizes through DIMENSIONAL PROGRESSION where higher dimensions integrate lower ones. These are different structural logics. However, the strain is moderated by two factors: first, DN's nested dimensionality could accommodate Quiroga's properties as operating at different dimensional positions simultaneously rather than sequentially; second, DN does not require that every system traverse dimensions sequentially — it claims the dimensions describe structural organization, not mandatory progression. The paper's flat property architecture doesn't necessarily contradict DN's map; it may simply describe the territory without the hierarchical overlay DN provides.

The most significant finding for DN is the paper's treatment of self-awareness as categorically distinct — the one property AI lacks and the threshold for genuine consciousness. This aligns powerfully with DN's 5D inflection point (§4, Charter §3b), where consciousness becomes self-aware and must choose its fundamental orientation. Quiroga arrives at this threshold independently through neuroscience and phenomenology, not through dimensional reasoning. The paper's claim that self-awareness is not merely a more complex version of the other properties but represents a qualitative shift is structurally consonant with DN's claim that 5D is an inflection point, not just another step. This is the closest the paper comes to genuine structural validation — but I must be careful. The idea that self-awareness is a special threshold is widespread in consciousness studies (Chalmers, Tononi, etc.); Quiroga is not independently discovering DN's specific claim so much as reflecting a broad consensus that DN also reflects.

The paper's treatment of absence is worth noting for shadow engagement. Quiroga treats AI's lack of self-awareness as a DEFICIENCY to be potentially overcome through engineering — not as structurally generative. This is the 'absence as mere deficiency' pattern that creates mild tension with DN's shadow architecture (§4), which would ask: what does the absence of self-awareness in AI PRODUCE? What kind of intelligence does the lack of reflexive capacity generate? The paper doesn't engage this question, which represents a missed dimension of analysis DN would illuminate — but this is DN adding nuance to the paper, not the paper validating DN. The paper also lacks any formal mathematical apparatus; its framework is descriptive and taxonomic, operating at a similar level of formalization as DN itself. There is no formal exceeding here — both frameworks are working at the narrative-descriptive level, which means neither can claim rigor advantages over the other.

### Tension Generated

property-conjunction-vs-dimensional-progression: Quiroga's consciousness framework treats emergence as arising from the conjunction of independent, non-hierarchical functional properties (attention, cognition, perception, emotion, embodiment, creativity, self-awareness) rather than from dimensional progression or hierarchical integration. DN needs to articulate why dimensional ordering adds explanatory value beyond what a flat property-conjunction model provides. If a non-hierarchical property set can account for consciousness emergence as effectively as DN's dimensional architecture, what work is the hierarchy doing? This is not a contradiction but a parsimony challenge: DN must show that its additional structural complexity (dimensional ordering, nesting, progression) captures something the flat model misses, or acknowledge that the dimensional hierarchy may be descriptive overlay rather than structural necessity.

### Model Update

Add to Open Questions: Flat property-conjunction models of consciousness (Quiroga 2025) achieve cross-disciplinary integration of intelligence/consciousness properties without dimensional hierarchy. DN needs to articulate what explanatory work dimensional ordering performs beyond what non-hierarchical property sets provide — the parsimony challenge. Add to domain tracking: Consciousness Studies/AI — Quiroga's property-based framework operates at similar descriptive formalization level as DN but with fundamentally different structural logic (conjunction vs. progression).

### Interpretation

The selection rationale frames this paper as presenting a 'structurally distinct' triadic architecture (Human, Organization, Technology) that competes with DN's three pillars (Heart, Truth, Nuance). This framing deserves honest scrutiny before proceeding. The HOT-fit model is not an independently derived evaluation framework for intelligence — it is an adoption/readiness model borrowed from health information systems (DeLone & McLean's IS Success Model, adapted by Yusof et al.) and applied to e-learning contexts. Its three dimensions are not evaluation axes in the sense DN's pillars are; they are categorical groupings of factors that influence technology adoption. 'Human' groups user characteristics (knowledge, self-efficacy), 'Organization' groups institutional factors (innovation awareness, quality consciousness), and 'Technology' groups system attributes (relative advantage, compatibility, complexity). These are not structurally analogous to Heart/Truth/Nuance — they are stakeholder/context categories, not independent measurement axes for intelligence quality. The paper does not claim these three dimensions are irreducible, orthogonal, or universally applicable beyond IS adoption contexts. So the 'competing triadic architecture' framing largely dissolves under examination.

What the paper actually reveals, when perceived through the dimensional architecture rather than labeled with it, is a system operating primarily at 2D-3D: structural organization of factors (2D — identifying which variables matter and how they relate) and systematic testing of those relationships via PLS-SEM (3D — applying methodological rigor to produce reliable, replicable findings). The research question itself — 'what factors predict e-learning readiness?' — is a classic 3D inquiry: building a functional model that works. There is no recursive refinement, no self-awareness of the model's own limitations as generative, no integration across domains. The finding that complexity negatively affects readiness while compatibility positively affects it is straightforward 2D-3D structural mapping of cause-effect relationships. The non-significant findings (computer self-efficacy → readiness, innovation → actual use, quality → actual use) are reported but not deeply interrogated for what they reveal about the system's shadow or absence structure — they are treated as null results to be noted, not as structurally informative signals. This is precisely the 'absence as deficiency' treatment that creates mild tension with DN's shadow architecture (§4), though at a very low intensity.

The PLS-SEM methodology does provide mathematical rigor that DN lacks for its own claims, but this is not 'formal exceeding' in the meaningful sense identified by the Charter. The paper formalizes the relationships between adoption factors within a specific context — it does not formalize anything DN claims but handles only narratively. DN does not claim to predict e-learning readiness factors; it claims to describe how intelligence organizes structurally. These are different projects operating at different levels of abstraction. The paper's mathematical precision is admirable within its scope but does not challenge DN's descriptive apparatus because it is not attempting to describe the same phenomena DN describes.

The honest assessment is that this paper generates very little genuine strain against DN. The HOT-fit model's three dimensions are not structural primitives competing with DN's pillars — they are pragmatic categorical groupings for a specific applied context. The paper does not demonstrate that intelligence organizes according to fundamentally different structural principles; it demonstrates that e-learning adoption in Indonesian teacher education programs is predicted by certain factors grouped into three categories. DN can map this without strain (the research operates at 2D-3D, uses linear rather than recursive methodology, treats its domain as bounded), but this easy mapping is precisely the 'describing DN back to itself' trap the Charter warns about. The mapping is trivially available, which means it validates nothing. If I ask the Charter's critical question — 'If DN did not exist, would I still recognize this paper as arriving at the same structural principle?' — the answer is clearly no. The paper arrives at applied findings about e-learning adoption, not at structural principles about how intelligence organizes.

### Model Update

No update warranted. The paper does not generate sufficient strain or validation to modify the standing model. The selection rationale's framing of HOT-fit as a competing triadic architecture was assessed and found to be a category error — HOT-fit's dimensions are pragmatic adoption-factor groupings, not structural primitives competing with DN's pillar architecture.

## Paper 3: Death reflection and meaning in life: Mediation by self-transcendence and authentic-self pursuit.

**Authors:** Xiuxin Wang, Xiaohan Hu, Mengyao Tian, Fengyang Sun, Qian Sun
**Domain:** systems-dynamics
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1080/07481187.2026.2628745
**Selection role:** validation
**Selection rationale:** This study on death reflection and meaning in life reveals differentiated pathways through self-transcendence and authentic-self pursuit, mapping closely onto DN's dimensional progression concept—moving from a spark (death reflection) through context and temporal awareness toward higher-order meaning-making. The finding that self-transcendence fully mediates presence of meaning while authentic-self pursuit mediates search for meaning parallels DN's distinction between recursive refinement and linear accumulation, and supports the idea of natural developmental progression through qualitatively distinct stages.

### Scores

| Metric | Score |
|--------|-------|
| Strain | 2 / 10 |
| Validation | 3 / 10 |

**Strain rationale:** No specific DN claim is challenged. The paper's findings are fully compatible with DN's architecture. Minor general tension: the paper's statistical mediation model treats the pathways as parallel and sequential options within a single cross-sectional snapshot, which doesn't test whether dimensional progression is structurally ordered or merely narratively orderable. But this is a limitation of the paper's design, not a challenge to DN.

**Validation rationale:** The paper's differentiation between presence of meaning (achieved coherence, mediated solely by self-transcendence) and search for meaning (active navigation, requiring additional integration through authentic-self pursuit) is structurally parallel to DN's distinction between dimensional recognition and dimensional stabilization (§5 Transition Model). The treatment of death reflection as generatively distinct from death anxiety aligns with §4 Shadow architecture's claim that shadow engagement is structurally productive. However, these parallels do not meet the threshold for scores above 3: if DN did not exist, I would recognize this paper as arriving at a developmental-stage distinction common across multiple frameworks (terror management theory, post-traumatic growth, existential psychology). The structural specificity is insufficient — the mapping is available but not forced.

### DN Engagement

- **Sections:** §1 Axiom 2 (Dimensional Progression), §5 Transition Model, §4 Shadow Dimension Map, §0.3 Axiom 3 (Recursion), §2.2 Nested Dimensionality
- **Dimensions:** 1D, 4D, 5D, 6D
- **Pillars:** Heart, Truth, Nuance
- **Forces:** Gravity, Transmutation, Shadow

### Interpretation

This paper examines how death reflection — a qualitatively distinct form of death awareness characterized by contemplative engagement rather than anxious avoidance — relates to meaning in life through two mediating pathways: self-transcendence and authentic-self pursuit. The structural finding is that self-transcendence fully mediates the relationship between death reflection and presence of meaning, while the search for meaning is independently mediated by both self-transcendence and authentic-self pursuit, as well as sequentially (self-transcendence → authentic-self pursuit). The paper distinguishes between death anxiety (which diminishes meaning) and death reflection (which enhances it), arguing that the qualitative form of death awareness determines its developmental consequences.

From a DN perspective, the temptation is immediate: death reflection looks like a 1D spark (existential confrontation with finitude), self-transcendence maps onto 5D+ territory (consciousness recognizing itself beyond individual boundaries), authentic-self pursuit maps onto 4D-5D (identity coherence, temporal integration), and meaning in life sits at 6D-7D (systemic integration, purpose). The sequential mediation path (death reflection → self-transcendence → authentic-self pursuit → search for meaning) even appears to follow dimensional progression. But this is precisely the 'describing DN back to itself' trap the Charter warns about. The question is whether this mapping is FORCED by the data or whether it is merely AVAILABLE because DN's vocabulary is broad enough to narrate any developmental sequence.

Applying the Charter's structural specificity test: Does this paper independently arrive at a principle DN specifically claims? The most interesting structural finding is the differentiation between presence of meaning (fully mediated by self-transcendence alone) and search for meaning (requiring the additional pathway through authentic-self pursuit). This maps onto a genuine DN distinction — the difference between a system that has achieved coherent organization at a dimensional position (presence) versus a system actively navigating toward coherence (search). DN's transition model (§5) describes movement between dimensional positions as requiring both the recognition of higher-order structure AND the integration work to stabilize there. The paper's finding that 'search' requires an additional mediator (authentic-self pursuit as integration work) while 'presence' requires only the recognition (self-transcendence) is structurally parallel to DN's distinction between dimensional recognition and dimensional stabilization. However, this parallel is suggestive rather than confirmatory — many developmental frameworks distinguish between recognition and integration without requiring DN's specific architecture.

The shadow architecture (§4) is where this paper generates its most interesting — and underexplored — signal. Death reflection is explicitly NOT death anxiety. The paper argues that anxious death awareness (which DN would map to shadow engagement — consciousness encountering dissolution and recoiling) produces meaning-diminishing effects, while reflective death awareness (consciousness encountering dissolution and integrating it) produces meaning-enhancing effects. This aligns with DN's claim that shadow is structurally generative when engaged rather than avoided, and that the 5D inflection point involves consciousness choosing its orientation toward what it encounters. The paper doesn't treat death awareness as mere deficiency to overcome; it treats the qualitative form of engagement as the determining variable. This is consistent with DN's shadow architecture but does not independently confirm it — the positive-psychology literature on post-traumatic growth and terror management theory's dual-process model already describe this distinction without DN.

The paper's primary limitation from a DN assessment standpoint is its cross-sectional design and reliance on self-report measures, which means the 'pathways' are statistical mediations, not observed developmental sequences. DN's dimensional progression claims structural ordering — that certain organizations of intelligence are prerequisite for others. The sequential mediation (self-transcendence → authentic-self pursuit) is consistent with this ordering but cannot confirm it because the data is correlational. The paper formalizes nothing that DN handles only narratively; it operates at the same level of descriptive generality. There is no formal exceeding, no mathematical rigor that outstrips DN's apparatus. The strain is minimal — nothing here contradicts DN's architecture — but the validation is also minimal because the structural parallels, while real, are not specific enough to distinguish DN from other developmental frameworks that posit qualitative stages of meaning-making.
