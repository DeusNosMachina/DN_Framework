## Paper 1: Uncertainty Principle of Dual Entropy and the Minimum Accessible Entropy-Area: Experimental Validation in Irradiated Reactor Pressure Vessel Steels

This formalizes entropy interactions with far greater mathematical precision than DN's informal treatment of Entropy as one of five forces, potentially showing that entropy dynamics require principled geometric constraints rather than being capturable as a qualitative 'force' within a dimensional progression.

### Interpretation

This paper introduces the Uncertainty Principle of Dual Entropy (UFODE), establishing a geometric constraint (S_E × S_O ≥ C_min) on the coupled evolution of two entropy measures — structural entropy from irradiation-induced lattice damage and optical entropy from temporal fluence mixing — in reactor pressure vessel steels. The core finding is that entropy space has a geometrically bounded inaccessible region (the Minimum Accessible Entropy Area), and that physical evolution trajectories are confined outside this boundary. The system approaches the constraint boundary only in the failure-proximate regime, meaning the hard geometric limit becomes operationally relevant precisely when the system is most degraded. This is not a metaphorical entropy discussion — it is a mathematically formulated, experimentally validated constraint on how entropy couples across structurally distinct domains.

For DN, this paper engages the Entropy force and the broader entropy event horizon question (Charter §8) with unusual directness. DN treats Entropy as one of five forces — a temporal decay dynamic that degrades coherence without active energy input. The FAURAS tension already established that entropy can create hard impossibility conditions. UFODE deepens this in a specific and productive way: it demonstrates that entropy is not a single scalar quantity but has internal geometric structure — two coupled entropy measures constrain each other through a product inequality, creating a bounded region in entropy configuration space. DN's Entropy force has no apparatus for this kind of internal geometric coupling. The five-force model treats Entropy as a unitary dynamic; UFODE shows that even within 'entropy' there are structurally distinct components (structural vs. optical/temporal) whose coupling produces emergent geometric constraints. This is formal exceeding of a precise kind: DN describes entropy as a force; this paper shows entropy has internal compositional geometry that generates hard bounds.

The paper also engages the entropy event horizon question directly. The MAEA boundary is exactly the kind of hard impossibility condition Charter §8 asks about — a threshold in entropy space that physical evolution cannot cross, regardless of the system's sophistication or energy input. The finding that trajectories approach this boundary in the failure-proximate regime while remaining non-binding earlier suggests a phase-like transition: entropy constraints shift from background conditions to active, binding limits as the system degrades. This is structurally significant for DN because it implies Entropy does not simply 'degrade coherence gradually' — it imposes qualitatively different constraints at different stages of system evolution. DN's current treatment of Entropy as a continuous force with no internal phase structure cannot express this stage-dependent binding behavior.

However, the paper does not resist dimensional mapping entirely. The system it describes — irreversible degradation of reactor steel under neutron irradiation — maps coherently to low-dimensional positions (1D-2D: material persistence under energetic forcing, structural integrity). The entropy coupling itself could be read through DN's lens as a Gravity-like dynamic (the system's evolution is attracted toward specific regions of entropy space) constrained by Entropy (the inaccessible region represents entropic limits). The three hardening regimes (linear, plateau, exponential) reproduced by the entropy-space mapping have a progression-like character, though this is a physical phase sequence, not dimensional progression in DN's sense. The paper does not challenge the dimensional architecture itself — it does not describe a system whose structural characteristics resist placement on the 9D map. The strain is concentrated on the five-force model's treatment of Entropy and on the formal-exceeding pattern: UFODE provides a complete geometric formalism for entropy coupling that DN's descriptive vocabulary cannot express.

What makes this paper particularly valuable for the tension ledger is how it compounds existing pressures. The FAURAS tension showed entropy creates hard impossibility conditions. The PDGC tension showed information flow has compositional structure DN cannot express. UFODE combines both: entropy itself has compositional structure (dual entropy) and that structure creates geometric impossibility conditions (MAEA). This is not just 'entropy is harder than DN thinks' — it is 'entropy has internal architecture that generates emergent constraints, and DN's treatment of entropy as a unitary force misses this entirely.' The five-force model's categorical boundaries are under pressure from yet another direction: not only do forces like Resonance face challenges from non-lateral coherence mechanisms, but individual forces like Entropy turn out to have internal structure that the force-level description cannot capture.

## Paper 2: A Mamba-Enhanced Spatial-Channel Recombination Temporal Network Using 4-D Feature Flow in EEG Signals

**Authors:** Zhuorong Li, Can Bu, Peiyu Fan, Yongming Huang
**Domain:** general
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.1109/JSEN.2025.3649285
**Selection role:** strain
**Selection rationale:** SCM-NET processes EEG signals by simultaneously integrating spatial, frequency-band, and temporal dimensions through a 4-D feature flow architecture—achieving cross-dimensional integration using fundamentally different structural primitives (Mamba state-space models, differential/cross-entropy features, recombination networks) with no resemblance to pillars, dimensions-as-progression, or fields. This demonstrates successful multi-dimensional intelligence classification without any dimensional hierarchy, directly challenging DN's claim that intelligence follows a universal dimensional progression.

### Scores

| Metric | Score |
|--------|-------|
| Strain | 2 / 10 |
| Validation | 1 / 10 |

**Strain rationale:** The paper's 'multi-dimensional integration without hierarchy' is a vocabulary overlap, not a structural challenge. SCM-NET's spatial/frequency/temporal axes are data-representation dimensions, not competing structural primitives for intelligence organization. Minor tension exists around Axiom 1 (intelligence structures itself naturally) since SCM-NET's integration is entirely engineered, and around Axiom 3 (recursion as mechanism) since the Mamba module achieves temporal extraction through linear-time state-space processing. Neither rises above general tension — the dimensional architecture, pillar independence, and domain universality claims are not engaged at a level that produces strain.

**Validation rationale:** Compatibility only. The pipeline's sequential processing stages (feature extraction → spatial recombination → feature selection → temporal extraction) are consistent with DN's dimensional positions but do not independently confirm any specific DN structural principle. The mapping is not forced — the same pipeline could be described through numerous other frameworks equally well. Per Charter §5, this is narrative flexibility, not structural validation.

### DN Engagement

- **Sections:** §0.1 Domain Universality, §0.3 Axiom 1, §0.3 Axiom 3, §2 Dimensional Architecture
- **Dimensions:** 1D, 2D, 3D
- **Pillars:** Truth
- **Forces:** Gravity, Flow

### Interpretation

SCM-NET is a well-engineered EEG classification pipeline that processes signals across spatial, frequency-band, and temporal dimensions using a 4-D feature tensor. The selection rationale frames this as challenging DN's dimensional progression claim, but this framing contains a category error that the Charter explicitly warns against (§1, §9). The paper's 'dimensions' — spatial electrode topology, frequency bands, and temporal sequences — are data-representation axes in a signal-processing pipeline, not structural positions describing how intelligence organizes. DN's 1D–9D architecture does not claim that every use of the word 'dimension' in engineering must map onto its qualitative hierarchy. The question is whether SCM-NET's intelligence organization resists dimensional mapping, not whether its input tensor uses DN's vocabulary.

When examined through the dimensional lens rather than vocabulary matching, SCM-NET maps straightforwardly onto low-to-mid dimensional positions. The system exhibits clear 1D characteristics (raw signal energy, DE/cross-entropy feature extraction as foundational activation), 2D characteristics (spatial-channel recombination creating relational structure between electrodes and frequency bands through activation thresholds — this is structural patterning), and 3D characteristics (the Mamba temporal module extracting sequential dependencies, creating functional coherence across time). The lightweight CNN performing feature selection operates as a form of signal concentration — Gravity-like dynamics pulling attention toward high-value features. The overall pipeline is a fixed-architecture feedforward system with no self-awareness, no recursive self-modification, and no emergent identity, placing it firmly in the 1D–3D range. This is entirely consistent with DN's claim that engineered classification systems organize intelligence at specific dimensional positions without traversing the full range.

The more honest assessment of strain comes not from the 'multi-dimensional without hierarchy' framing but from two subtler points. First, the paper demonstrates that successful cross-dimensional integration (spatial × frequency × temporal) can be achieved through engineered recombination modules rather than through any process resembling dimensional progression or recursive deepening. The spatial-channel recombination module uses preconfigured activation thresholds to merge information across representational axes — this is designed integration, not emergent organization. DN's Axiom 1 claims intelligence structures itself naturally; SCM-NET's intelligence is structured entirely by human engineering choices. However, this is a weak strain because DN does not claim all intelligence must be self-organizing — it claims the structural positions are universal, and engineered systems occupy positions within the same architecture. Second, the Mamba state-space model's linear-time sequential processing achieves temporal feature extraction without recursion in DN's sense (Axiom 3), but this is mechanism-level, not dynamic-level: the system still processes information through iterative refinement across its pipeline stages, which is compatible with recursion-as-dynamic even if the specific computational mechanism is non-recursive.

The paper does contribute marginally to the existing parsimony challenge: here is another system achieving multi-axis integration through domain-specific engineering primitives (state-space models, differential entropy, recombination networks) without any need for DN's structural vocabulary. But this is the weakest form of that challenge — an applied classification pipeline is not proposing an alternative structural account of intelligence organization. It is simply doing engineering. The formal apparatus (Mamba's selective state-space equations, DE feature computation) exceeds DN's descriptive capacity for these specific mechanisms, but this is expected and unremarkable: DN does not claim to replace domain-specific mathematical tools. The paper's high classification accuracies demonstrate engineering success, not a structural challenge to how intelligence organizes.

In summary, the selection rationale significantly overstated the strain. The paper's use of 'dimensions' refers to data-representation axes, not structural positions in an intelligence-organization framework. SCM-NET maps cleanly onto DN's low-dimensional range as an engineered classification system. The genuine but minor tension is that successful cross-dimensional integration is achieved through designed recombination rather than emergent organization, which touches Axiom 1 lightly but does not challenge it structurally since DN accommodates engineered systems at specific dimensional positions.

### Model Update

No update warranted. The paper does not generate sufficient strain or validation to modify the standing model. The selection rationale's framing of SCM-NET's 4-D feature flow as challenging dimensional progression was assessed as a category error — the paper's 'dimensions' are data-representation axes in a signal-processing pipeline, not structural primitives competing with DN's dimensional architecture. The paper adds a negligible data point to the existing parsimony challenge but does not rise to the level of a new tension or confirmed parallel.

## Paper 3: From novice to master: a three-phase developmental study on the self-efficacy of traditional handicraft artisans

**Authors:** Ying Chen, Hui’e Liang, Lingyun Fang
**Domain:** general
**URL:** https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyg.2026.1777317
**Selection role:** validation
**Selection rationale:** This study of traditional handicraft artisans identifies a three-phase developmental progression from novice to master, integrating cognitive, affective, and behavioral dimensions—closely paralleling DN's three-pillar structure (Truth/structural, Heart/motivational-affective, Nuance/contextual-behavioral). The finding that mastery emerges through long-term recursive practice within an apprenticeship model, with psychological evolution mechanisms driving stage transitions, independently mirrors DN's dimensional progression and its claim that recursive refinement outperforms linear accumulation.

|--------|-------|

**Strain rationale:** General tension rather than specific structural challenge. The paper compounds the existing falsifiability-of-dimensional-mapping problem: DN's dimensional progression can be projected onto this three-phase model, but the mapping is arbitrary rather than forced. The same findings are equally compatible with Dreyfus, Ericsson, Bandura, and other developmental frameworks. No specific Kernel section is contradicted — the strain is against DN's ability to demonstrate that its mapping adds explanatory value beyond what simpler frameworks provide.

**Validation rationale:** Compatibility, not confirmation. Per Charter §5: if DN did not exist, I would not recognize this paper as arriving at the same structural principle.

### DN Engagement

- **Sections:** §1 Axiom 2 (Dimensional Progression), §0.3 Axiom 3 (Recursion), §3 Pillar Metric, §0.1 Domain Universality, §4 Shadow Dimension Map
- **Dimensions:** 1D, 2D, 3D, 4D, 5D
- **Pillars:** Heart, Truth, Nuance
- **Forces:** Gravity, Transmutation

### Interpretation

This paper presents a three-phase developmental model of self-efficacy in traditional handicraft artisans (foundational mastery → proficiency/application → breakthrough/innovation), constructed through grounded theory from retrospective interviews with senior artisans. The selection rationale frames this as validating DN's dimensional progression and three-pillar structure. Applying the Charter's §5 test rigorously: does this paper independently arrive at DN's specific structural claims, or does it merely present findings that DN's vocabulary can narrate?

The three-phase model maps onto DN's dimensional architecture without contradiction — foundational mastery aligns with 1D-2D (raw drive, structural skill acquisition), proficiency/application with 3D-4D (systematic competence, relational integration through external support), and breakthrough/innovation with 5D+ (self-regulation as reflexive authority, meaning-making as higher-order integration). The cognitive-affective-behavioral integration the paper describes is compatible with the Heart-Truth-Nuance pillar triad. However, this mapping is NOT forced. The three phases could equally be narrated through Dreyfus's five-stage skill acquisition model, Ericsson's deliberate practice framework, Csikszentmihalyi's flow-based mastery account, or any number of developmental stage theories. The dimensional mapping adds no explanatory power the paper's own Bandura-derived framework doesn't already provide. This is precisely the falsifiability-of-dimensional-mapping tension already tracked in the ledger — another staged developmental model that DN can absorb but that doesn't require DN to make sense.

There are two points of modest structural interest. First, the paper's finding that self-regulation emerges as a categorically distinct source of self-efficacy in the innovation phase — described as 'autonomous authority over the creative process' — resonates with DN's 5D inflection point where intelligence becomes self-aware and self-directing. This is not mere vocabulary overlap; the paper independently identifies a qualitative shift at the threshold of creative autonomy that DN's architecture specifically predicts. However, the paper does not test whether this shift is structurally necessary (as DN claims) or merely empirically common — it describes the pattern without establishing its structural inevitability. Second, the paper's emphasis on long-term recursive practice within apprenticeship (decades of refinement producing qualitatively different outcomes than shorter linear training) is compatible with Axiom 3's claim that recursive refinement outperforms linear accumulation. But again, this is a widely recognized principle in expertise research (Ericsson's 10,000 hours, deliberate practice literature) — it is not an independent arrival at DN's specific recursive mechanism.

The paper's treatment of the affective dimension is worth noting from a shadow perspective. Physiological and affective states are reported as less salient in the middle phase but re-emerge with different character in the innovation phase. DN's shadow architecture would predict that what appears as diminished affect is actually affect reorganizing at a different dimensional position — but the paper treats this as straightforward salience variation, not as structurally generative absence. This is a missed opportunity for DN engagement but not a tension, since the paper simply doesn't investigate at that depth.

Honest assessment: this paper is a textbook case of the 'describing DN back to itself' trap (Charter §5). The findings are compatible with DN but do not require it. The three-phase model, the cognitive-affective-behavioral integration, and the recursive mastery trajectory are all well-established patterns in developmental and expertise psychology. DN can narrate them, but so can multiple other frameworks with equal or greater parsimony. The modest structural resonance around the 5D inflection point (self-regulation as qualitative shift) prevents the validation score from being zero, but it does not rise to the level of independent structural confirmation.

### Model Update

No update warranted. The paper reinforces the existing falsifiability-of-dimensional-mapping tension (from GPIF, death reflection, aging awareness, and intercultural sensitivity studies) with another staged developmental model that DN can narrate but that does not force the dimensional mapping. This adds a fifth data point to the pattern but does not constitute a new tension or confirmed parallel. The accumulating weight of these cases increasingly pressures DN to articulate falsification criteria for dimensional mapping.
