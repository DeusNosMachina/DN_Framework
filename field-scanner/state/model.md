# DN Field Scanner — Framework Standing Model (v3)

*This document tracks the DN Framework's cross-disciplinary standing as assessed*
*through the lens-based interpretation paradigm. It is a living document that*
*evolves with each cycle.*

*Initialized: v3 charter launch*

## Seed State

The DN Field Scanner v3 begins with context inherited from 15 cycles (50 papers)
under the v2 charter. Key findings from that period:

### What v2 Established

1. **DN's descriptive generality is confirmed.** Every paper examined could be
   narrated through DN vocabulary. This is a feature of breadth, not evidence
   of predictive power.

2. **Formal exceeding is the primary strain pattern.** Multiple papers
   (PDGC, Kuramoto, pseudo-coherence, UFODE, DPE) formalize phenomena DN
   describes only narratively. The mathematical apparatus in these papers
   exceeds what DN can express.

3. **The force model faces completeness pressure.** Dynamics that don't cleanly
   map to any of DN's five forces have been identified (topology-restructuring
   mediation, pseudo-coherence without resonant coupling).

4. **The parsimony challenge is live.** Alternative frameworks achieve similar
   organization with fewer or different components (active inference, flat
   property-conjunction, homogeneous-dimensional models).

5. **Flow is undifferentiated.** DN's Flow concept lacks the compositional
   structure that real information dynamics exhibit (redundancy, synergy,
   unique causal components, spectral organization).

6. **Validation ceiling was 3/10.** No paper scored above 3 on the old
   validation axis. This was a measurement problem, not necessarily a
   framework problem — the v2 rubric asked whether papers independently
   arrive at DN's specific claims, which is an unreasonably high bar for
   a meta-framework.

### What v3 Tests Differently

The v3 charter shifts from "Does external research confirm DN?" to "Does DN's
lens produce structural insight when applied to external research?" This directly
tests the meta-positional claim: DN as a dimensional grammar for decomposing
intelligence across domains.

The inherited tensions from v2 remain active and will be tracked. The v3 lens
paradigm may reframe some of them — e.g., the parsimony challenge becomes "Does
DN's lens add value that simpler lenses do not?" rather than "Is DN's architecture
necessary?"

### Coverage Gaps from v2

- **Dimensions 6D–9D** were rarely engaged. Most papers operated in the 1D–5D range.
- **Heart pillar** was underrepresented. Most engagement was Truth-led.
- **Shadow architecture** was rarely tested at depth beyond surface-level mapping.
- **Lens yield** was never measured (the concept didn't exist in v2).

v3's paper selection strategy (1 strain / 1 lens / 1 high-dimensional) is
designed to address these gaps.

### Cycle Update — 2026-04-02

**Topology as a Language for Emergent Organization in Complex Systems: Multiscale Structure, Higher-Order Interactions, and Early Warning Signals** (strain, strain: 7, lens_yield: 4)

The 'formal exceeding' pattern from v2 should be upgraded to 'formal alternative': topology is not merely formalizing what DN describes informally—it provides a complete alternative structural grammar for emergent organization across domains, with its own primitives, operators, and cross-domain applicability. This strengthens the parsimony challenge. However, DN's shadow architecture and pillar triad remain genuine structural additions that topology lacks, suggesting DN's unique value may lie in its evaluative/orientational dimensions rather than its organizational detection claims. The strain ceiling should be updated: this is the strongest 'successful alternative' case encountered, scoring 7 against foundational claims.

**Toward an Emergent Paradigm for Neurodiversity and Health** (lens, strain: 2, lens_yield: 5)

First lens yield score above 3 in v3. The yield was produced by two specific DN architectural elements: pillar independence (revealing conflation in ESF's biopsychosocial triad) and shadow symmetry (distinguishing organized-toward-limitation from environmental mismatch). Shadow architecture continues to be DN's strongest candidate for producing lens yield that other frameworks cannot — this is now supported by both the v2 shadow engagement findings and this v3 lens application. The pillar decomposition's relationship to the biopsychosocial model warrants tracking as a new tension.

**Mimesis and the Durational Becoming: Assimilation and Emergent Behavior in Art** (high-dimensional, strain: 2, lens_yield: 5)

First high-dimensional paper under v3. Heart-led engagement confirmed as underserved but productive. Shadow architecture produced genuine lens yield in an aesthetics domain—this is a positive data point for DN's shadow symmetry as a unique contribution. The 5D inflection point proved structurally useful for analyzing orientation choices in creative practice. Performative aesthetics is a viable domain for DN lens application, though the informality of the source material limits how testable the structural insights are. Lens yield of 5 is the highest recorded under v3 so far and was driven by shadow architecture and forced dimensional ordering—both 'mature' tier elements.
