### ZONE C: Completion Criteria Cards

**Purpose:**

Completion Criteria (formerly "Definition-of-Done") formalize what "done" means for a session's work or an exercise's deliverable. They are a short, agreed-upon checklist that must be true for a deliverable, decision, or idea to count as finished. In dimensional work, this ensures clarity, shared expectations, and measurable completion across all perspectives.

**Why It Matters:** Without a shared success definition, teams risk *drift* â€” where work appears complete but lacks alignment, quality, or strategic coherence. Completion criteria act as a dimensional anchor, capturing the essential conditions for closure that match the *type* of thinking and action being done.

**Component Parts of a Completion Criteria Card:**

1. **Artifact/Decision** â€” What the criteria apply to (e.g., "Vision Statement," "Customer Journey Map").
2. **Owner** â€” The single person who signs off.
3. **Checklist** â€” 3â€“7 conditions that must all be true before the work is complete.
4. **Dimension Tag** â€” Color code to match the originating dimension.
5. **Date/Review Trigger** â€” When these criteria should be rechecked or revised.

**Status Evaluation:** At session close, each defined criterion is evaluated using these statuses:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Met** | All conditions satisfied. |
| **Partially Met** | Some conditions satisfied; specifics noted. |
| **Unmet** | Conditions not satisfied. |
| **Deferred** | Carrying to next session â€” creates a forward reference. |
| **Scope Changed** | Session evolved and the original criterion no longer applies. Note what changed and why. |
| **Not Evaluated** | Facilitator chose not to evaluate (ran out of time, deprioritized). |
| **Open Exploration** | Session has no pre-defined completion target. Valid facilitation choice â€” the system still captures what was produced but does not evaluate against criteria. |

Completion criteria are diagnostic instruments, not bureaucratic gates. Criteria often evolve mid-session or remain unresolved at close. The session always closes regardless of criteria status. Unmet and deferred criteria surface in the next session's change rationale prompt.
